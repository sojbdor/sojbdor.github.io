Red Carpet Incubator, Modular research

KRKRKR is a podcast series created by Sukkube during Corona times on Sphere Radio (Leipzig, Germany), with a one-hour episode every month, recorded in my studio during the era of the coronavirus pandemic. This podcast is dedicated to showcasing both new releases and unreleased techno tracks, spanning the spectrum from unfamous productions to experimental sounds. These tracks have been collected over many years of research into the world of experimental techno, rave culture. Techno has always served as a genuine form of communication for me, a means to found a place in ourself for creativity and care against the forces of neoliberalism and capitalism. The menu of KRKRKR serves as a journey through cerebral soundscapes and fat liquids. This is not your typical electronic music experience; it's a sonic rebellion against the capitalist influences that mold our world. In a society driven by profit, we aim to discover illumination within the shadows. Rather than being a simple dance mix, the radioshow opening a space for reflection and exploration through the intricate depths of this world

Sans Blabla on Campus Radio Toulouse

Sojbdor was the first entity of Sukkube. Experimental house music to techno. Once a month on Sundays at 10:00 PM. No one truly knows which planet Sojbdor hails from. He doesn't speak often, generally having nothing to say. When he wanders alone, Sojbdor takes his headphones to listen to music while exploring the world, whether it's on a bike, skateboard, or scooter. He gets so absorbed in the music that sometimes he loses track of his surroundings. He might find himself lost in thoughts, dancing alone, whether it's on the street, at home, or even at work. Sojbdor heads to clubs to listen to his favorite artists. He go towards contemporary techno, hard-techno, dub-techno, modern techno, groovy, and experimental sounds, although he occasionally enjoys ambient, rock, metal, hip-hop, or rap. He realized that music transports him into the future, yet it also depends on his moods because sometimes it stirs up feelings of nostalgia in him. Sojbdor isn't sure if he decides to feel nostalgic himself or if it's the music that inspires this sentiment
